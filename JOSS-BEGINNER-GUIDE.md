# JOSS REVIEW CHEAT SHEET

**Simple guide for beginners - what to expect and how to handle it**

---

## 📋 **What JOSS Reviews Look Like**

### **You'll Get:**
- **2-4 reviewers** looking at your work
- **Long checklists** they need to check off
- **Public discussion** on GitHub (everyone can see)
- **Mix of technical and scientific questions**

### **Common Questions:**
- "Can I install your software easily?"
- "Are your examples clear?"
- "Is your documentation complete?"
- "Does your paper explain things well?"

---

## 💡 **Simple Response Strategy**

### **Step 1: Don't Panic!**
- JOSS reviewers are trying to **help you improve**
- They're not trying to reject your paper
- Most comments are fixable

### **Step 2: Divide and Conquer**
- **Takahashi**: Handle anything about code, installation, technical stuff
- **Shimizu**: Handle anything about science, methodology, research
- **Together**: Handle writing, examples, big picture stuff

### **Step 3: Simple Response Format**
```
Thank you for this feedback!

We've addressed this by [what you did].

You can see the changes in [filename or commit].

Let us know if this resolves your concern.
```

---

## 🎯 **What Reviewers Love to See**

✅ **Clear examples** that work  
✅ **Easy installation** instructions  
✅ **Good documentation** with explanations  
✅ **Polite responses** that show specific changes  
✅ **Quick fixes** for small issues  

---

## 🚫 **What NOT to Do**

❌ Don't argue with reviewers  
❌ Don't ignore their questions  
❌ Don't make long complicated responses  
❌ Don't forget to show what you changed  
❌ Don't work on the same comment at the same time
